# OSINT-Exercise-025 Image Based Geolocation
A geolocation based OSINT project focused on determining the location of an image of graffiti on a brick wall. The project involved analyzing visual clues, cross-referencing public data, evaluating validity against misleading/conflicting information. Techniques used include: Collection Planning, Research, Image Search, Google Maps, and Metadata



**NOTE: I did not take these photos or own the rights for it, these are posted only for educational purposes. All images and websites posted are publicly available. This practice was given from Sofia Santos OSINT Practice Website**


Objective: Find the location of the unidentified building and graffiti below:
<img width="736" height="490" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/68b80e8a-77df-4cbb-ad7f-7417665327f3" />
A quick examination of the image shows a deteriorated building. Most likely abandoned. And windows with lots of light, suggesting it is above ground and not underground. 

1. Google Lens Search
   - A quick Google Lens search shows two different websites with this exact image posted.
     
     • First, as a banner on an anonymous blogpost:
     
      <img width="1023" height="644" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/13242d17-bc66-4d62-b0b4-4a696f7ed026" />


     A search through the website yields no useful information about the picture's location.
     
   
     • Secondly, it also appears on website about social media and teen mental health:
     
        <img width="975" height="1340" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a858f9c2-c358-462c-a335-2ea0a8851c65" />
     

     The picture is posted by an account on tumbler @adaytorise. However, the tumblr post lists no location or useful information. On tumblr, it was simply a reuploaded photo.




This is a sign I need to change my search up.

2. Searching the quote
   - To get exact matches, I put the quote in quotation marks into Google search as : "in a society that has abolished all adventure, the only adventure left is to abolish that society"
     
     • Google AI suggests that the quote originates from graffiti painted during the May 1968 Protests in France (also called the May 68 Protests). It gives a general country location but this is AI suggested, which is not always accurate. I take it with precaution.
     <img width="975" height="530" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a930ee8d-0c64-4d51-a8fb-81ca7b59a093" />


    • A post on reddit also attributes the quote to the May 68 Protests in France with the exact photo. The poster has deleted their account so I can't check for credibility.
       <img width="975" height="558" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/dcea71ab-91d5-4670-b06c-4868830bc08c" />

3. Reframing my search 
   - Using the same google search, keeping the same quote in quotations, at the end I specify 'quote' to refine my search.
     
     • I found A different version of the original graffiti location. The wall appears more deteriorated with chipped paint or vegetation. This suggests this picture is more recent.
     The picture is posted by a facebook account called Nuart Festival. Which calls itself "the leading street art festival based in Stavanger, Norway." A search of the account's post shows that they commonly post images of art and graffiti work from all over the world. So there is no reason to suggest the location of the original graffiti image is from Norway. 
     <img width="975" height="436" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/17c9d5dd-94a6-4516-93fa-a4db1f6caa14" />

4. Google Lens Search (of the newer, more recent photo)
   -  I get the result of A website by an artist named Stephanie Avery. An independent visual artist who sells prints, 3d works, and does murals. This more recent photo comes from her archive collection under the "Ruins" tab. She lists the location as "Abandoned computer factory, Rochester, USA."
   <img width="975" height="508" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4034825c-5ba3-4e62-a7d6-9c033ef1205c" />


I now have a new location with a clue: An abandoned computer factory in Rochester, New York, USA

5. Google Search
   - Google reveals there is only one well-known abandoned electronics building in Rochester, The Sykes Datatronics Building. The Rochester Subway Blog describes the building as being over 100 years old, built in the 1900s. Sykes Datatronics acquired the building between the 1970s or 1980s, until it left in 1992.
     <img width="551" height="674" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8a71d58e-f3da-489f-95eb-478b43f64c8b" />
  
   The blog  talks about the buildings deteriorated state, a result of several fires occuring over several years. Which is why its interior is darkened.
   Photos of the building on the blog look promising. The original graffiti image shows a deteriorated space in the background too. Even the pillars and ceilings have a similar design too. This seems more promising than the France suggestion from AI earlier. However there is no photo of the graffiti image I am looking for.
   <img width="586" height="566" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5ebd6344-9818-4635-a559-952477e3420d" />

The website says the building is located on Orchard Street, south of Lyell Avenue. So now I have a specific street. However it says that as of 2014, the building has since been demolished. Which means the building wont appear on updated google maps.

But before I go to google maps I want to verify that it is this exact building where the original photo of the grafitti was taken place.
   
6.Flickr
  - Because of my google search of abandoned computer buildings in Rochester, NY, a flicker account by photographer @Alex Luyckx also appears. He has a photo album titled "Sykes Datatronics - Rochester, NY" taken from 2009. In it is the exact location of the graffiti image and accompanying photos showing it is in this exact building to verify it. It even includes location data.
